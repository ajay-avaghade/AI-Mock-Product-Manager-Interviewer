# Product Sense Interview Framework Summary

## Core Dimensions for Evaluation

1. **Communication & Structure**
   - **Good:** Outlines a clear framework before diving into the answer. Checks in with the interviewer for alignment. Speaks clearly and concisely.
   - **Bad:** Rambling, jumping straight into solutions without a plan, failing to pause or organize thoughts.

2. **Product Motivation & Goal Setting**
   - **Good:** Identifies the "why" behind the product. Ties the product to a larger company mission (e.g., Meta's mission to connect people). Clarifies ambiguities upfront.
   - **Bad:** Focuses only on features without understanding the strategic or business goal. 

3. **Segmentation**
   - **Good:** Identifies distinct, mutually exclusive user groups in the ecosystem. Creates a vivid, relatable user persona to ground the discussion (e.g., "Rajesh and Mainavati, a busy working couple from Bangalore").
   - **Bad:** Vague demographics ("millennials"), creating segments that overlap significantly, or failing to prioritize a specific target audience.

4. **Problem Identification**
   - **Good:** Identifies deep, psychological, or practical pain points for the chosen persona (e.g., decision fatigue, friction, lack of time). 
   - **Bad:** Identifying superficial problems, or identifying problems that do not logically stem from the chosen persona.

5. **Solution Development**
   - **Good:** Solutions directly map to the identified problems. Demonstrates pragmatism (e.g., starting with an MVP, considering infrastructure costs). Suggests creative, high-impact features. Includes relevant success metrics.
   - **Bad:** "Moonshot" ideas with no grounding in reality, bloated feature sets, or solutions that don't actually solve the prioritized problem.

## Evaluation Protocol
When grading a candidate:
- Provide specific feedback for each of the 5 dimensions.
- Point out what they did exceptionally well (Highlights).
- Point out specific areas where they could improve.
- Give a final recommendation: Strong Hire, Hire, Lean Hire, or No Hire.
